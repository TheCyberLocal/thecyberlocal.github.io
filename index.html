About Me

Only a challenge is worthwhile.

I am a passionate and self-driven Software Development Engineer with a strong background in computer science and mathematics. Before attending App Academy, I worked as a Remote CAD Specialist for Modular Furniture Group and as a Stock Analytics Software Developer. One of my proudest achievements was formulating a morphing algorithm to run market simulations and detect the most profitable trading pattern.

My journey toward software engineering began at Modular Furniture Group, where I was drawn to IT beyond my CAD responsibilities. Fascinated by the ability of software to automate IT processes, I transitioned from programming as a hobby to pursuing my first software development contract. At App Academy, I significantly improved my ability to write maintainable and scalable code while building collaboration and communication skills. I thrive on challenges and continuously seek opportunities to learn and grow, with notable projects including STRling, a modern interface to RegEx, and MyTube, a full-stack application for organizing YouTube content.

Three technical skills I bring to software development are proficiency in JavaScript and Python (including frameworks such as React, Redux, Flask, and Express.js), experience with database design and management (including SQLAlchemy, PostgreSQL, and SQLite), and a strong understanding of DevOps practices and tools (including Docker, AWS, and GitHub). My non-technical skills include self-accountability, strong diligence, critical problem-solving, and quick learning.

My proudest project is STRling, which showcases my creative skills and how I learned to publish packages on PyPI and NPM. My favorite part of the development process is designing and implementing elegant and scalable architectures that ensure long-term maintainability. Outside of tech, I enjoy philosophical conversations, learning fun facts in any science or mathematics, and engaging in high-paced, strategic competitions. What sets me apart from other candidates is my unique passion for continuous learning and my philosophical mindset, which drives me to pursue and overcome the most difficult challenges analytically rather than avoid them.

Regarding my quote on challenges, allow me to explain:

  • When I take on a task that is not challenging, there is a likely chance of success with no sense of accomplishment and a small chance of failure with internal embarrassment. This means a likely neutral feeling from success and potential negative feeling from failure.
  • However, when I face a challenge, I have a less likely chance of success but with a sense of accomplishment and a more likely chance of failure but the shame diminishes. This means a potential positive feeling from success and a more likely but less shameful failure.

Only the challenge has potential for greatness, and this is why I am and will forever be a lifelong learner striving to overcome the next challenge with unwavering determination so I can once again earn the sensation of another victory.
